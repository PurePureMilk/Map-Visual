Python Scripts for Mapping Drought Percentiles in the US and Globally

The scripts below map out the wetness percentile of three hydrological variables: surface soil moisture, root zone soil moisture, and groundwater. Surface soil moisture measures the percentage of water (in volume) in the top two centimeters of the soil. Meanwhile, root zone soil moisture represents that quantity in the top one meter of the soil. Groundwater here represents shallow groundwater storage. All three variables vary in response to precipitation and evapotranspiration. If there is a lot of precipitation, they may all increase but increases in groundwater may lag changes in other two variables. During summer or droughts, soil moisture and groundwater may decrease due to evaporation or lack of precipitation. Higher or lower percentiles are used to indicate wetter or drier conditions in these variables. For additional information and data download, please visit the NASA GRACE or GRACE FO-based drought indicator website (https://nasagrace.unl.edu/).
